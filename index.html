Prep Guide

How to Use This Guide

  • Buy proteins first and portion them before refrigerating or freezing.
  • Quantities on the shopping list are rounded to practical store sizes.
  • The dinner-to-next-lunch system means you only cook one extra portion each evening — no separate lunch cooking needed.

Sunday Prep Session — approx. 90 minutes

1. Portion proteins

Meat, fish, and mince portions are listed as raw weight before cooking. Tuna is listed as drained weight, and dairy items are listed as served weight.

  • Chicken breast → 2 × 170g raw portions (Day 1 dinner & Day 2 lunch) + 2 × 180g raw portions (Day 4 dinner & Day 5 lunch)
  • Beef mince → 2 × 170g raw portions (Day 2 dinner & Day 3 lunch)
  • Hake → 2 × 180g raw portions (Day 3 dinner & Day 4 lunch)
  • Salmon fillets → 2 × 160g raw portions (Day 5 dinner & Day 6 lunch)
  • Sirloin steak → 2 × 170g raw portions (Day 6 dinner & Day 7 lunch)
  • Chicken breast → extra 180g raw strips for Day 7 dinner

2. Wash and chop vegetables

  • Chop broccoli, carrots, butternut, courgettes, peppers, onions, and green beans
  • Wash salad leaves; store with paper towel in a sealed container
  • Slice cucumber; prep beetroot if not using a pre-cooked pack
  • Keep mushrooms and tomatoes separate for quick breakfasts

3. Cook base carbs

  • Brown rice → 2 × 100g cooked portions
  • Basmati rice → 2 × 100g cooked portions
  • Quinoa → 2 × 100g cooked portions for Days 4 & 5 lunch; cook 2 more on Day 5 evening for dinner & Day 6 lunch
  • Pre-boil or roast sweet potatoes and baby potatoes for the first half of the week
  • Measure dry oats into containers for overnight oats (Day 3) and shake add-in (Day 6)

4. Breakfast and snack pre-packing

  • Boil 2 eggs for Day 2 snack
  • Build overnight oats for Day 3 breakfast — assemble in a jar the night before
  • Portion Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, almonds, Provitas, and rice cakes into grab-and-go servings
  • Keep fruit washed and visible for easier adherence

Midweek Top-Up — approx. 45–60 minutes (Day 4 or evening before)

  • Roast potato wedges for Day 6 if not done earlier
  • Prep gem squash and extra salad vegetables
  • Marinate sirloin steak and chicken breast strips for Day 7
  • Refill snack portions for the second half of the week

Storage Notes

  • Store cooked proteins and starches in sealed containers and use within 2–3 days
  • Freeze raw proteins you will only use later in the week; thaw in the fridge the day before
  • Keep salads and roast vegetables separate from dressings until serving
  • Label leftover containers by day to reduce decision fatigue

Adherence Tips

  • If hunger is high, increase non-starchy vegetables first
  • If training volume is higher on a specific day, move a fruit serving to pre- or post-workout
  • If a USN shake day is not practical, swap with a Greek yogurt-based meal from another day and keep the rest of the structure the same
  • Meat, fish, and mince portions are 150–180g raw weight before cooking; vegetable portions are generally 250–300g

Kelfit Overview Summary

Goal

This plan is designed for sustainable fat loss: enough protein to support muscle retention, enough carbohydrates to keep training and daily energy practical, and enough vegetable volume to improve fullness without making the plan feel restrictive.

Nutrition Structure

  • Protein appears at every main meal and most snacks.
  • Meat, fish, and mince portions are listed as raw weight before cooking; tuna is drained weight, and dairy is served weight.
  • Carbs are moderate and portioned: cooked rice or quinoa is generally 100g, potatoes are 140–150g, and oats are 30–45g.
  • Vegetable portions are intentionally large, usually 250–300g with lunch and dinner.
  • Higher-calorie extras such as peanut butter, avocado, nuts, seeds, feta, hummus, salmon, steak, and olive oil should stay measured.

Meal Prep System

  • Dinner is cooked as two portions where practical, with the second portion becoming lunch the next day.
  • USN Diet Fuel Ultralean is included on selected days for convenience, not as the foundation of the whole plan.
  • Provitas, rice cakes, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, fruit, eggs, and biltong provide repeatable snack options.
  • Airfryer and non-airfryer meals are both included so the plan stays usable across different cooking setups.

Fat-Loss Adjustment Rule

  • If hunger is high, increase non-starchy vegetables first.
  • If energy is low around training, move a fruit or carb portion closer to the workout instead of adding extra snacks.
  • If weight is not trending down after 2–3 consistent weeks, reduce one calorie-dense extra or slightly reduce a carb portion before cutting protein.
  • If weight loss is too fast or adherence feels poor, add a small carb or yogurt-based snack and keep the meal structure consistent.
