Ladies Kelfit Meal Plan

7-Day plan • High protein • Controlled carbs • Large vegetable portions • No carbs at supper • SA-friendly foods

About This Plan

The Ladies Kelfit Meal Plan is a structured, high-protein eating framework designed around South African foods. It combines controlled carbohydrates, large vegetable portions, and healthy fats to support body composition, sustained energy, and adherence — without complicated calorie counting.

🍳 5 meals a day Breakfast → AM Snack → Lunch → PM Snack → Supper
💧 Protein at every meal Preserves muscle & keeps hunger low
📈 Carbs at breakfast & lunch only No starch or fruit at supper — ever
🌿 Big vegetable portions Aim for 250–300g at lunch & supper
⏳ Cook-double system Tonight’s supper = tomorrow’s lunch
🇬🇧 SA-friendly foods All ingredients available at major SA retailers

7-Day Meal Plan

Daily Milk Allowance: 1 cup fat-free milk • OR • 2 cups unsweetened Almond Breeze / oat milk / soy milk — use for coffee, tea, or oats. Sweeteners allowed: stevia, erythritol, Suki, or xylitol only.
How to read this plan:
  • Each day follows the same structure: Breakfast → AM Snack → Lunch → PM Snack → Supper.
  • Supper has no starch/carb — protein + allowed vegetables + 2 fat choices only.
  • All protein weights are raw / uncooked unless noted as “drained” (tinned fish) or “as-served” (dairy).
  • Vegetables at lunch and supper should be a large portion — aim for 250–300g.
  • If you are still hungry, add more allowed vegetables — never reduce protein.

Quick Recipe — Pan-Seared Salmon
  1. Pat salmon dry and season with lemon juice, garlic, dried dill or mixed herbs, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat a non-stick pan with 1 tbsp olive oil. Sear skin-side down for 3–4 min, then flip for 2–3 min until just cooked through.
  3. Lightly steam green beans and spinach; add cherry tomatoes to the pan in the last minute to warm through.
Meal Prep Tip Cook double tonight — save the second salmon fillet for Saturday’s lunch (flaked over brown rice and salad). Reheat salmon in a covered pan over low heat for 3–4 min.

Quick Recipe — Grilled Ostrich Steak
  1. Season ostrich with garlic, black pepper, a pinch of thyme or mixed herbs, and a little salt (ostrich is lean — do not over-season).
  2. Grill or pan-sear on high heat, 2–3 min per side for medium; ostrich can be served slightly pink.
  3. Rest for 3–5 min before slicing. Serve with a generous fresh salad and avo slices.
Meal Prep Tip Cook double tonight — save the second ostrich steak for Sunday’s lunch (sliced over butternut and salad). Ostrich can be served cold or warmed briefly in a pan.

Quick Recipe — Grilled Chicken & Roasted Vegetables
  1. Season chicken breast with garlic, lemon juice, mixed herbs, salt, and pepper.
  2. Grill on medium-high heat 6–7 min per side until cooked through (internal temp 74°C). Rest for 3 min before slicing.
  3. Toss broccoli, courgette, and onion with a light spray of oil, salt, and pepper. Roast at 200°C for 20–25 min until slightly charred. Drizzle 1 tbsp olive oil over veg to serve.
Meal Prep Tip Cook double tonight — save the second chicken breast for Monday’s lunch next week. Sunday is ideal for batch-cooking rice or quinoa and prepping salad jars for the week ahead.

Quick Recipe — Satay-Style Ostrich Mince
  1. Brown ostrich mince with diced onion, garlic, and sliced mushrooms in a non-stick pan.

Quick Recipe — Bolognese Mince (No Pasta)
  1. Brown beef mince with 1 tbsp olive oil, diced onion, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add 1 can chopped tomatoes (400g), 1 tsp dried mixed herbs, and a pinch of chilli flakes. Simmer 12–15 min until thick.

Important reminders:
  • No starch or fruit at supper — protein, allowed vegetables, and your 2 fat choices only.
  • Choose 2 fats per supper from the options listed on each card. Do not exceed 2.
  • Alcohol counts as 1 fat choice and is allowed a maximum of once per week (1 glass dry wine 120ml or 1 tot spirits with soda/diet soda).
  • If progress stalls after 2 weeks, reduce portion of starch/carbs slightly — never reduce protein.
  • Rotate this plan with a second week of different meals to keep variety and adherence high.

140g strawberries, blueberries, raspberries or mixed — fresh or frozen, lowest sugar option 200g watermelon (cubed) — larger volume, very refreshing in summer 180g fresh pineapple (cubed) — great with yogurt or cottage cheese
Pear / Peach / Nectarine
1 large pear — filling due to fibre; great afternoon choice 1 large peach — seasonal; use yellow cling or freestone variety 1 nectarine — slightly sweeter than a peach, same portion 1 large mango slice (~120g) — higher sugar; enjoy occasionally, not daily
Kiwi fruits (2)

Tip: fruit variety matters
Rotate fruits daily to get a wider range of vitamins and fibre types. Frozen berries are a great budget-friendly option — portion and thaw as needed.

Alternative Meal Ideas

Swap out any day in the plan with ideas below. Same rules apply: starch at breakfast / lunch only, no carbs at supper, protein at every meal, cook double for the next day’s lunch.

☀️ Breakfast Ideas
  • Overnight Oats: 40g oats + 200g fat-free yogurt + 10g chia seeds + 1 scoop whey + sliced fruit. Prep the night before.
  • Egg Muffins: 3 eggs + chopped spinach + tomato + lean bacon bits in a muffin tin at 180°C for 15 min. Make 6 at once — keeps 3 days.
  • Protein Smoothie Bowl: 1 scoop whey + 150g frozen berries + 80ml almond milk blended thick; top with 30g oats and sliced fruit.
  • Greek Yogurt Bowl: 200g plain fat-free yogurt + 35g high-protein granola + 100g berries + 10g pumpkin seeds.
  • Rye Toast & Eggs: 2 slices rye bread + 3 poached or scrambled eggs + baby spinach + sliced tomato.
  • Pronutro Porridge: Wholewheat Pronutro prepared per packet with fat-free milk allowance + sliced banana or berries.
☀️ AM Snack Ideas
  • Rice cakes + cottage cheese with cucumber slices and a pinch of paprika.
  • Provitas + lean biltong (sliced, not sticks).
  • Hard-boiled egg + rye crackerbread — prep 3 eggs at a time; keep in fridge.
  • Hummus + raw veg sticks (carrot, cucumber, celery) + 4 rice cakes.
  • Shaved chicken + Ryevita — roll the chicken in the cracker.
  • Protein shake — Sport RX or whey with water on busy mornings when prep time is zero.
☀️ Lunch Ideas
  • Quinoa Power Bowl: 100g cooked quinoa + 140g grilled chicken + roasted peppers, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, mixed leaves.
  • Tuna & Avo Wrap: 1 brown wrap + 120g tinned tuna (drained) + 30g avo + shredded lettuce + tomato.
  • Chicken Lettuce Cups: Large iceberg leaves filled with spiced chicken mince, cucumber, and grated carrot. Use butternut as the carb on the side.
  • Lentil & Salmon Bowl: 100g cooked lentils + 130g tinned or grilled salmon + large mixed salad + lemon dressing.
  • Beef Mince Rice Bowl: 100g brown rice + 120g beef mince in tomato sauce + large green salad.
  • Sweet Potato & Egg Salad: 120g roasted sweet potato + 2 hard-boiled eggs + large salad + 1 tbsp olive oil dressing.
☀️ PM Snack Ideas
  • Yogurt + fruit: 120ml plain fat-free yogurt + any allowed fruit portion.
  • Protein shake + fruit: Sport RX or whey blend with ice and water + 1 piece of fruit.
  • Cottage cheese + fruit: 150g fat-free cottage cheese + sliced strawberries or diced pineapple.
  • Rice cakes + peanut butter: 2 rice cakes + 20g sugarfree peanut butter (counts as your fat choice).
  • Grapefruit + shake: Half a grapefruit + Sport RX — great mid-afternoon energy reset.
☀️ Supper Ideas (no carbs)
  • Chicken Stir-Fry: 150g chicken strips with broccoli, peppers, mushrooms in a light soy-ginger sauce. No rice — serve over a large bed of baby spinach.
  • Beef Mince Lettuce Cups: 120g spiced beef mince in large iceberg lettuce leaves with cucumber, grated carrot, and a yogurt-garlic drizzle.
  • Prawn & Avo Salad: 140g grilled prawns on mixed leaves, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and 60g avo.
  • Baked Egg & Veg Frittata: 3 eggs + spinach, mushrooms, diced tomato, lean bacon baked at 180°C for 15 min. Top with 50g feta.
  • Ostrich Mince Bowl: 120g ostrich mince with onion, garlic, and herbs + 300g roasted cauliflower and broccoli + 60g avo.
  • Salmon & Cucumber Salad: 130g pan-seared salmon on a large salad of cucumber, dill, cherry tomatoes, red onion — dressed with lemon juice only (salmon provides the natural fat).
Rules when swapping:
  • Keep the same type of food in each slot — protein for protein, carb for carb.
  • When swapping to a higher-fat protein (e.g. salmon instead of hake), reduce the portion by 10–20g.
  • All vegetable swaps are free — keep the 250–300g target per meal.
  • Supper must never include a starch/carb — even when using a meal idea swap.
  • Always apply the cook-double principle: make 2 portions at supper, use the second for tomorrow’s lunch.
